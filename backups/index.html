Our Story

Derrick Gregory grew up in Chesapeake, Virginia, close to where Interstate 64 ends. He moved to Richmond, Virginia in 2007 when he transferred from Old Dominion University in Norfolk to Virginia Commonwealth University. He graduated from VCU in 2009 with a degree in urban studies and geography.

Kelly Hall grew up in St. Peters, Missouri, not far from the other end of Interstate 64. She graduated from Truman State University in Kirksville, Missouri in 2010 wiht a degree in Interdisciplinary Studies: Human Geography and a minor in Environmental Studies.

Derrick and Kelly’s mutual interest in cities, plants, biking, and traveling blossomed into a relationship.

In 2010, Derrick was searching for career options and was encouraged by a professor to re-enroll in Virginia Commonwealth University for a 1-year GIS Certificate. At the same time, Kelly was also figuring out career options and was offered a graduate assistantship at VCU by the same professor.

Over the summer, Kelly happened to stay at Derrick’s friends house while looking for apartments in Richmond. The room was open because the friend was traveling in Indonesia with Derrick.

On Wednesday, August 18, 2010, Kelly and Derrick met at a meet-and-greet school orientation for their programs. Kelly had only been in Richmond for 3 days. That Friday, Derrick invited Kelly out on a bike ride to Chimborazo Park, a vegetarian dinner at Ipanema Cafe, and then to Bollywood Night.

Derrick and Kelly’s mutual interest in cities, plants, biking and traveling blossomed into a relationship.

On June 8, 2013, Derrick proposed to Kelly at Chimborazo Park.
